A rational expression is a fraction usually with variables in it.

Something like  
$$
\frac{x + 2}{x^2 - 3}
$$
is a rational expression.

---

### Reduce to lowest terms

1. Factor numerator and denominator  
2. Cancel common factors

---

### Multiply & Divide

Multiply → multiply numerators & denominators  
Divide → multiply by reciprocal

Example:

$$
\frac{\frac{x^2 + x}{x + 4}}{\frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 16}}
=
\frac{x^2 + x}{x + 4} \times \frac{x^2 - 16}{x + 1}
$$

---

### Add & Subtract

We first find a common denominator.

Example:

$$
\frac{3}{2x + 2} + \frac{5}{x^2 - 1}
$$

LCD:

$$
2(x+1)(x-1)
$$

So:

$$
\frac{3}{2(x + 1)}\cdot\frac{x - 1}{x - 1}
+
\frac{5}{(x + 1)(x - 1)}\cdot\frac{2}{2}
=
\frac{3x + 7}{2(x + 1)(x - 1)}
$$
